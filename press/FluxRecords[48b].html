The partials are now controlled using the k5's internal methods
whereby I represent 2 knobs to make an endless dial
which in turn moves all odd, even, octave and 5th harmonics up or down.

An angle knob gives a linear preference to upper or lower harmonics (or none)
so traditional overtones shapes can be obtained.

This produces spectra that I can during play shift in real-time
and it is very K5 specific
so it brings out the specificity of the instrument.

Whatever remains of the Monte Carlo Method?
Why does it work with pitch
sometimes with articulation
but generally not with timbre?

It appears that the 12-tone method's original intent
was not its later development in total serialization
but to free music from tonality
and to hold pitches in an equilibrium
so that the law of large numbers
would eventually reveal all possible combinations.
